Outdoor Essentials

You will need something to carry all the essentials you might need during a day.

The sun is extremely strong in Southeast Asia and prolonged exposure to UV rays can seriously damage your eyes, especially if you’re used to being in dull, rainy England. We advise that you invest in some decent sunglasses back home that protect your eyes from UVA and UVB rays.


This is non-negotiable no matter where you’re travelling to, but it’s even more important to think about ahead of time because sunscreen comes at a much higher cost in Southeast Asia. Stock up on sunscreen at home for a better value.

If you are traveling to somewhere rains a lot, we recommend ones that comes with Goretex lining, they can prevent water from getting insede of youe shoes and keep your feet dry, but they are not quite breathable. Therefore, if you are traveling to Southeast Asia, ones that are more breathable are better choice and they dry faster.


Carrying a portable power supply can prevent your electronic devices from dying, but do pay attention on the capacity because there is a regulation on what type of power supply you can carry on the flight. Normally 10000mAh is way more that enough for daily use, and you can have 3-4 charges before it runs out.


Medical

Having at least a basic first aid kit handy is crucial. In Asia’s humidity, cuts, burns and grazes can become infected really easily and it’s essential to take care of the wound so that it doesn’t lead to a bigger problem. Anti-septic wipes, alcohol, iodine and anti-septic cream are a must, as well as decent plasters (band-aids) and bandages to cover the cut up. A good first aid kit also comes with a thermometer which is the best and first way to check if you’re coming down with a fever.

Aloe vera gel is able to disinfect and help healing all kinds of wounds
